LEGAL LAUNCH CHECKLIST — METALWORK JOBS

1. Confirm the exact identity and contact details of the data controller for every vacancy before candidate data is forwarded.
2. Confirm whether Priyantha, an employer, a recruitment provider, or more than one party acts as controller/joint controller/processor.
3. Match the EU Recruitment Data Notice to the real workflow, recipients, legal basis and retention period.
4. Do not request passports, IDs, visas, medical records, criminal records or banking data during the first enquiry.
5. Use a separate optional choice for future-vacancy contact. Never pre-select it.
6. Delete or anonymise candidate data when the recruitment purpose ends, unless a documented lawful reason justifies retention.
7. Do not add analytics, advertising pixels or marketing cookies without an appropriate consent mechanism.
8. Confirm rights to use all logos, profile artwork, photos and job-offer content.
9. Keep visa wording factual: no recruiter, employer or intermediary can guarantee a consular decision.
10. Obtain a review by a qualified Polish lawyer or data protection professional before relying on the site as a complete legal compliance solution.
